The Tree & Wheel of diet

How To Interact

  • Select Nutrient View:
    • Use the radio buttons labeled "Tree" and "Radial Cluster" to switch between different visualizations of the nutrient data.
  • Tree View:
    • Choose "Tree" to view the nutrient data represented as a hierarchical tree structure.
    • In the tree view, each node represents a different nutrient category or subcategory.
    • This visualization represents a comprehensive breakdown of various essential nutrients found in food sources. The graph is structured as a node tree, with each node representing a specific nutrient category such as Protein, Carbohydrates, Fats, Minerals, and Vitamins. Within each nutrient category, there are subcategories representing different sources of that nutrient. For instance, under Protein, we have categories like Animal Protein, Plant Protein, and Vegan Protein. Each of these subcategories further breaks down into specific food items such as Chicken, Salmon, Lentils, Tofu, and more. Each food item node includes two key pieces of information: the amount of the nutrient (in grams) present per 100 grams of that food item and the energy content (in kilocalories) per 100 grams.
      By exploring this graph, one can quickly identify the nutrient content of various food items and understand how different nutrients are distributed across different types of foods. It provides a visual guide to making informed decisions about dietary choices, highlighting the richness of nutrients in different food sources.
  • Radial Cluster View:
    • Select "Radial Cluster" to switch to a radial cluster representation of the nutrient data.
    • In the radial cluster view, each concentric circle represents a different nutrient category or subcategory.
    • The size of each arch within the circles corresponds to the average quantity of that nutrient in the diet.
    • Compare the sizes of the arches to understand the relative proportions of different nutrients in the diet.
    • This radial cluster visualization offers a unique perspective on the nutrient composition of a diet, resembling a series of concentric pie charts. Each "slice" or "arch" in the radial cluster represents the average quantity of a specific nutrient present in the overall diet. Imagine the center of the radial cluster as the core of a pie chart, with the first circle representing the nutrient categories such as Protein, Carbohydrates, Fats, Minerals, and Vitamins. Moving outward from the center, each subsequent circle or layer represents subcategories within those nutrient categories, such as Animal Protein, Plant Protein, Vegan Protein, Simple Carbs, Complex Carbs, and so on. Within each segment, the size of the arch reflects the average quantity of that nutrient present in the diet. For example, a larger arch for Protein indicates a higher average amount of protein consumed, while a smaller arch for Fats suggests a lower average intake of fats. By examining the radial cluster as a whole, one can quickly grasp the relative proportions of different nutrient categories in the overall diet. For instance, if the outer circle representing Animal Protein has larger arches compared to the circle for Plant Protein, it indicates a higher average consumption of animal-based proteins in the diet. This visualization provides an intuitive way to understand the balance of nutrients in one's diet at a glance. It allows for easy comparison between different nutrient categories and highlights areas where adjustments might be needed to achieve a more balanced and nutritious diet.
  • Exploring Data:
    • Use both views to gain insights into the nutrient composition of the diet.
    • Switch between the tree and radial cluster views to compare different aspects of the data.
    • Interact with the graphs to explore specific nutrient categories, their sources, and average quantities.
    • Clicking on nodes in the tree view or hovering over arches in the radial cluster view provides more detailed information.
  • Analyzing Trends:
    • Notice how the graphs change based on the selected nutrient view.
    • Analyze trends such as which nutrient categories dominate the diet, the sources of specific nutrients, and the average quantities consumed.
    • Use these insights to make informed decisions about dietary balance and adjustments for a healthier lifestyle.
